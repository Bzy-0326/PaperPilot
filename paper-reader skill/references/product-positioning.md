# Product Positioning

## What Paper Reader is

Paper Reader is a research-paper discovery assistant for learning, topic exploration, and implementation follow-through.

Its job is not just to find papers. Its job is to help a user quickly decide which papers are:
- relevant to the current topic
- worth reading first
- practical to reproduce or follow
- accessible enough for a newcomer to start with

The intended product expression is:
- recommendation cards on the homepage
- structured decision support on the detail page
- reproducibility evidence surfaced as first-class information

## Core user value

Paper Reader should reduce wasted reading time.

It helps the user judge:
- whether a paper is worth deeper reading
- whether a paper is easy or hard to reproduce
- whether there is enough evidence to trust the recommendation
- whether the paper is a good next step for a current project topic

## UX framing

Homepage solves:
- what should I look at today
- which papers deserve priority
- which ones fit my current topic best

Detail page solves:
- why this paper was recommended
- whether there are code, datasets, appendix clues, or other implementation evidence
- whether the work looks beginner-friendly or reproduction-friendly

## Product guardrails

Prefer:
- explicit reasons over vague scores
- evidence-backed reproducibility judgments
- relative comparison within a topic
- beginner-friendly phrasing when possible

Avoid turning the product into:
- a generic paper list
- a plain abstract summarizer
- a UI that hides why a paper ranked highly
